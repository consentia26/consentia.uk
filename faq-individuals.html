FAQ – Frequently Asked Questions

Questions and guidance for individuals whose personal data or photos have been shared.

What should I do if my private photos were stolen?

Check which photos were shared, capture evidence, and contact your hosting service or the authorities immediately.

How can I quickly remove stolen personal data online?

Use the platform's reporting tools, contact support, and document everything. We can guide you step by step.

My account was hacked and my photos were published. What should I do now?

Change your passwords, secure your accounts, and follow our instructions to report and remove the content.

How do I report a website or social network sharing my photos without permission?

Every platform has a reporting form. We provide detailed guidance to make sure your requests are processed quickly.

How long does it take to remove stolen photos online?

It depends on the platform, but by following the correct steps, some photos can be removed within 24–48 hours.

Can I get legal help if my private photos were shared?

Yes, in the UK, there are legal procedures to remove content and protect your rights. We guide you through the process.

What if a company refuses to remove my stolen data?

You can submit an official request. We provide instructions and templates for urgent removal.

Can I recover my stolen photos?

In some cases, yes. We guide you to secure your data and limit further distribution.

My photos have been shared on multiple sites. What should I do quickly?

List the sites, capture evidence, and start the removal process as soon as possible. We help prioritize the most urgent sites.

How can I prevent my private photos from being stolen in the future?

Secure your accounts, enable two-factor authentication, limit sharing, and follow our practical advice to reduce risk.

What should I do if my photos were taken without my consent?

Contact the platform or website immediately, document evidence, and secure your accounts. We guide you to protect your rights.

My photos were taken without my knowledge. Is this a violation of my rights?

Yes, even if GDPR doesn't cover every case, UK privacy laws apply. We show how to report and remove this content.

What should I do if my personal data has been leaked online?

Identify the platforms, document evidence, and exercise your GDPR rights. We provide templates and advice for urgent removal.

How to react quickly if my photos or personal data are already public?

Prioritize high-traffic sites, capture evidence, contact platforms, and use official removal requests to accelerate the process.
