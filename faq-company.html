FAQ – For Companies

Answers to common GDPR questions for businesses.

Why does my company need a GDPR consultant?

GDPR compliance is mandatory in the UK and EU. Non-compliance can lead to fines and legal actions. We provide expertise to ensure your business follows the rules correctly.

What should I do if my company experiences a data breach?

Identify the affected systems, secure the data, document the breach, and notify the relevant authorities. We guide you step by step.

How can I prevent future data breaches?

We help implement internal policies, staff training, and technical measures to reduce risks and strengthen your company’s GDPR compliance.

What are the common GDPR mistakes companies make?

Mismanaging consent, lacking data protection policies, insufficient staff training, and ignoring data subject requests. We identify these issues and fix them.

What is the process for handling customer data requests under GDPR?

Data subjects can request access, correction, or deletion. We help your company respond efficiently and legally to all requests.

Can a company be fined for not complying with GDPR?

Yes, fines can be substantial. We provide preventive audits to help companies avoid penalties.

How quickly should I respond to a data breach?

Immediate action is critical. Notify the relevant authorities within 72 hours if required, and secure affected data. We provide clear procedures to act fast.

Do I need a GDPR policy for my company?

Absolutely. A clear policy helps employees follow the rules and demonstrates compliance to regulators. We can create or review your policies.

What if my company receives a GDPR complaint?

We help manage complaints professionally, document all steps, and communicate with the authorities to minimize risks.

Can Consentia train my staff on GDPR compliance?

Yes, we provide tailored training sessions for staff, managers, and IT teams to ensure your company remains compliant.

Our customer database has been leaked online. What should we do immediately?

Secure your systems, document what data was exposed, notify affected individuals if necessary, and contact us for step-by-step guidance to mitigate legal and reputational risks.

A hacker gained access to our company data. How fast do we need to act?

Immediate action is critical. Contain the breach, secure accounts, document everything, and notify authorities within 72 hours if required. We provide a clear emergency protocol.

We received a legal notice about GDPR non-compliance. What now?

Don’t panic. We help analyze the notice, prepare your response, and implement corrective actions to limit fines or legal consequences.

Some employees accidentally shared personal data. How should we respond?

Identify the scope of the exposure, document it, inform affected parties if needed, and apply preventive measures. We guide you to correct the situation fast.

How can we quickly verify our company is GDPR compliant before an audit?

We provide an urgent compliance check, identifying gaps, high-risk areas, and immediate fixes to ensure your company passes audits.

We suspect a third-party provider mishandled data. What steps should we take?

Audit the provider’s practices, secure your contracts, notify authorities if necessary, and implement stricter data protection policies. We can assist with every step.

Our company suffered multiple small data breaches. How do we manage them?

Document each incident, prioritize the most urgent, mitigate risks, and implement systematic changes. We provide structured action plans to reduce future breaches.
